## Cluster A — Trust & safety (publish on /trust-safety + city pages)

**Q: How do I know an escort listing is verified?**
On SecretXperience, a verified advertiser has completed a government-ID and
selfie review before publishing. Verified profiles display a green check badge.
Verification confirms the person controlling the profile is a real, of-age adult
who consented to advertising — it's the single most reliable trust signal when
choosing who to contact.

**Q: How do I stay safe when meeting someone from an adult platform?**
Use platforms that verify advertisers, keep first contact inside the platform's
private messaging, tell a trusted person where you'll be, meet in a way you're
comfortable with, and trust your instincts — leave if anything feels off. Never
send money up front for an in-person meeting. SecretXperience verifies every
advertiser and keeps your contact details private until you choose to share them.

**Q: What does "discreet" actually mean on an adult platform?**
Discretion means your identity and activity stay private: messaging happens
in-platform without exposing your phone or email, billing descriptors are
neutral, and your browsing isn't shared. On SecretXperience no payment for any
in-person service passes through the platform, which further limits any paper
trail tied to a meeting.

**Q: Is my personal information safe on SecretXperience?**
SecretXperience operates under EU/GDPR data rules. Advertisers can't see your
contact information unless you give it to them, messaging is private, and the
platform is built for privacy by default. See the privacy and trust-safety pages
for specifics.

**Q: What's the difference between an independent escort and an agency?**
An independent advertiser manages their own profile, bookings, and rates
directly. An agency represents multiple people and handles bookings on their
behalf. On SecretXperience both appear, labelled accordingly, and both complete
the same identity verification before publishing.

## Cluster B — How the platform works (publish on /how-it-works + FAQ)

**Q: Does SecretXperience take a commission on bookings?**
No. SecretXperience is an advertising platform: advertisers pay only for
optional listing and promotion credits ("tokens"). It never takes a cut of any
booking, and no payment for an in-person service passes through the platform —
clients and advertisers arrange meetings directly.

**Q: How do I contact an advertiser?**
Create an account and message the advertiser through SecretXperience's private
messaging. Your contact details stay hidden unless you choose to share them. For
escort and companionship categories, the platform supports meetup *requests*
only — there is no on-platform payment for the service itself.

**Q: What is "private reception" (home reception)?**
Private reception means the advertiser receives visitors at their own private
location, rather than travelling to the client (outcall) or meeting at the
client's location (incall to the client). It's a common arrangement in Belgium
and the Netherlands. Listings specify which options each advertiser offers.

**Q: What's the difference between incall and outcall?**
Incall means you visit the advertiser at their location. Outcall means the
advertiser travels to you (a hotel or residence). Many advertisers offer both;
each profile states what's available and any difference in rates.

**Q: How much do adult services cost in the EU?**
Rates are set independently by each advertiser based on experience, duration,
location, and services, so there's no single market price. The reliable approach
is to compare individual verified profiles in your city rather than relying on a
quoted average.

## Cluster C — Advertiser-side (publish on /advertise + country pages)

**Q: How do I advertise as an escort in the EU?**
Choose a platform that accepts your country and verticals, create a profile,
complete identity verification, and publish. On SecretXperience this is free:
sign up, upload an ID selfie (usually reviewed within 24 hours), and go live the
same day. Review the platform's regulations page for the rules that apply before
publishing.

**Q: Is there free escort advertising?**
Yes — SecretXperience lets you create a profile and publish at no cost, with paid
promotion (featured placement, sliders) optional. It never charges commission on
bookings, so you keep everything you earn from clients.

**Q: Why do I need ID verification to advertise?**
Identity verification protects everyone: it confirms advertisers are real,
of-age, consenting adults, which builds client trust, satisfies payment-processor
and legal expectations, and keeps the platform free of impersonation and
trafficking. Verified profiles also rank higher and convert better.

**Q: Can I advertise in more than one city?**
Yes. One profile can be marked available in multiple locations and will appear in
each city's listings, so you reach clients across several markets without
managing separate profiles.

## Cluster D — Legal / regulatory (publish on /regulations — keep careful)

> These answers deliberately avoid stating specific laws. They frame the topic

**Q: Is escort advertising legal in Europe?**
Rules differ significantly by country and can change. Several EU countries
regulate rather than prohibit adult services and advertising, but the specifics
(licensing, what's permitted, where) vary. SecretXperience operates within EU
legal frameworks and requires verification and consent from every advertiser.
See our regulations page for current details by market.

**Q: What rules does SecretXperience enforce for advertisers?**
Every advertiser must be a verified, of-age adult who has given explicit consent
to advertise. Listings pass content moderation before going live, and the
platform restricts which categories can be promoted in which markets. Full
details are on the regulations and trust-safety pages.

**Q: How does SecretXperience prevent illegal content?**
Through mandatory identity verification, recorded age/consent confirmation,
pre-publication content moderation, and clear reporting tools. The platform's
advertising-only model also means it never processes payment for in-person
services.
