# 01 — Marketing Audit Summary

**Overall: 58/100 (Grade C)** — a pre-launch *marketing-readiness* score. The
product is a B+ build; the distribution engine barely exists. That gap is the
whole story.

## Score breakdown

| Category | Score | Key finding |
|----------|-------|-------------|
| Content & Messaging | 68 | Sharp "discreet/verified/premium" positioning; thin long-form & trust content |
| Conversion Optimization | 62 | Strong flows (verify, tokens, booking) but no urgency/social proof at launch |
| SEO & Discoverability | 55 | Good meta/schema foundation; programmatic pages exist but were thin (now fixed) |
| Competitive Positioning | 60 | Compliance/advertising framing is a real moat; under-communicated publicly |
| Brand & Trust | 64 | Verification + 2257/DMCA/trust pages strong; no third-party reputation yet |
| Growth & Strategy | 30 | No audience, no growth loop, no distribution engine — **the critical gap** |

## The core diagnosis

You're in the "build it and they will come" trap. The product has verification,
live streaming, tiered ads, a discover-swipe page, 8 categories, and a clean
compliance story. None of that generates demand on its own.

**Why owned/earned is the entire growth model here:** paid search (Google Ads)
and most paid social (Meta, TikTok) prohibit adult services. So the channels
that work are the ones you own or earn:
- **SEO** (programmatic city × category) — your biggest lever
- **AEO** (getting cited by ChatGPT/Perplexity) — wide-open first-mover niche
- **Content engine** (one pillar → many posts)
- **Newsletter acquisition** (buy a warm audience instead of building from zero)
- **Free tools** (the tool markets itself)
- **Viral artifacts + referral** (users/advertisers market for you)

## Biggest strengths to amplify (don't rebuild — market louder)

1. **Compliance framing** — "we're an advertising platform; no service payment
   touches the site." It's literally what's unlocking your processor
   conversations. Almost nobody in the category can say it. Put it everywhere.
2. **Trust infrastructure** — mandatory ID verification, 2257, DMCA,
   trust-safety, regulations pages. Above-average for the vertical. This is the
   "safe, verified" position competitors lack.
3. **Build quality** — the site looks and works better than typical directories.
   Screenshots of it are themselves a marketing asset.

## Biggest gaps (the roadmap targets these)

1. No audience → newsletter acquisition + content engine
2. Thin programmatic pages → fixed this session, expand to all categories
3. No AEO presence → publish the content pack
4. No launch urgency/social proof → founding-advertiser scarcity
5. No growth loop → referral + shareable artifacts
