# 07 — Newsletter Acquisition Playbook

The video's Strategy 6: instead of building an audience from zero (slow, and
platform-risky in adult), **buy or partner a niche newsletter** and inherit a
warm, trusting audience on day one. This is the fastest warm-audience play
available to you, and email can't be deplatformed the way social can.

## Why this fits SecretXperience especially well
- Paid acquisition is largely closed to you → owned audience is gold.
- Your monetization is advertiser-side, so a newsletter audience of
  adult-curious / lifestyle / nightlife readers converts on **both** sides:
  clients to browse, and potential advertisers to list.
- A newsletter is a direct line you control — exactly what a privacy-sensitive,
  deplatforming-prone vertical needs.

## What to look for
| Criterion | Target |
|---|---|
| Size | 5,000–50,000 subscribers (don't overbuy early) |
| Niche | EU adult/lifestyle/nightlife/dating, ideally BE/NL/DE/FR |
| Engagement | Open rate > 25–30%, real clicks (ask for proof) |
| List health | Low bounce, GDPR-consented list, double opt-in if possible |
| Price | Often €5k–€20k for ~10k subs; many small ones make €0–500/mo and will take an offer |

## Where to find them
- **Duuce.com** — marketplace of newsletters for sale (the video's source).
- **Substack / beehiiv / Ghost** — search the niche; DM owners directly.
- **X/Twitter** — search "[niche] newsletter"; many independents.
- **Existing adult/lifestyle blogs** with an email capture — approach even if
  not officially "for sale".

## Two deal structures (offer both)
1. **Acquisition** — buy the list + brand outright. You own it. Cleanest, but
   confirm GDPR consent transfers (see due diligence).
2. **Revenue-share / sponsorship-to-acquire** — start by sponsoring/placing
   SecretXperience content, prove it converts, then offer to acquire. Lower risk,
   builds the relationship first. Good when the owner is hesitant to sell.

## Outreach script (cold DM / email)

> Subject: Quick question about {{newsletter_name}}
>
> Hi {{name}} — I read {{newsletter_name}} and really like what you've built in
> the {{niche}} space. I run an EU platform in an adjacent space and I'm looking
> to reach an engaged audience like yours.
>
> Two quick questions: (1) would you ever consider selling the newsletter, or a
> partnership? (2) if so, could you share rough numbers — subscribers, open rate,
> and how the list was built (consent)?
>
> No pressure at all — just exploring. Happy to start with a paid placement so
> we can both see how it performs before anything bigger.
>
> Thanks, {{your_name}} · support@secretxperience.eu

## Due diligence before paying (don't skip)
- **Consent & GDPR:** how was the list built? Double opt-in? Can consent legally
  transfer to a new owner? Get it in writing.
- **Proof of engagement:** screenshots of the last 5 sends (opens, clicks,
  unsubscribes), not just a subscriber count.
- **List hygiene:** bounce rate, % active in last 90 days, any purchased/scraped
  contacts (walk away if so).
- **Deliverability:** sending domain reputation, spam complaint rate.
- **Content fit:** will their audience welcome SecretXperience, or feel
  bait-and-switched? Plan a warm intro, not an abrupt pivot.

## After acquisition
- Warm-introduce yourself to the list before any hard promotion.
- Keep delivering the content they subscribed for; weave SecretXperience in.
- Plug into your sequences (`05`): client engagement + advertiser acquisition.
- Repeat across niches once the first one proves the model (the video's point).
